15.1 Multivariate Normal Distribution

Corollary 15.1 If \(\boldsymbol{Y} \sim N(\boldsymbol{0}, \sigma^ 2I)\), and \(Q\) is an orthogonal matrix, then \(Q^T\boldsymbol{Y}\) still follows multivariate normal distribution with mean \(\boldsymbol{0}\) and variance-covariance matrix \(\sigma^2 I\)

\[ \mathbb{E}(Q^T\boldsymbol{Y}) = Q^T\mathbb{E}(\boldsymbol{Y}) = \boldsymbol{0} \]

\[ \text{Var}(Q^T\boldsymbol{Y}) = Q^T \text{Var}(\boldsymbol{Y})Q = \sigma^2 I Q^TQ = \sigma^2I \]
